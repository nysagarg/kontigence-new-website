<Web Dev>

The participants would need to make a website based on a topic. The website will be presented for 10-15 minutes over live call and stock resources will be provided to all participants.

Due to an overwhelming response, registrations for this event has been Closed

Letter to the Participants

Dear Participants,

We are honoured to welcome you to Kontingence’s Web Dev- an opportunity to outshine your competitors with skilful mastery of website development. We expect this event to serve as a pinnacle of creativity and innovation, with functionality and aesthetics as fundamental pillars of support.

In a world where online marketing is reaching the pinnacle of influence, it is important to be able to represent an organisation or yourself as an individual. A website is an easy-to-access medium of information, and through this event, we aim to inculcate basic, if not advanced, web developing skills. We put forward the Web Dev as a stage of talent and ingenuity, hoping to see participants dive into the world of marketing and brand presentation.

This occasion is explicitly structured as a stage for creative individuals with the focal spotlight on helping them improve their insight and sharpen their abilities. There are of course, certain factors that one should keep in mind while enrolling in this event. While some experience would definitely be helpful, we encourage everyone to come and learn something new!

May the deserving participants win!

Team Kontigence

Guidelines

The participants will need to participate in a team of three to five members. The teams will be required to make a website based on a theme given by the organizers. A time period 48 hours would be given to all participants for the design, development and testing of their website.

Any framework or programming language can be used for this event; however, If the project requires a Database only one of the following will be allowed to be used: MySQL, SQLite, Firebase or MongoDB. Some frameworks and programming languages that we recommend are listed below.

HTML

CSS

Javascript

Angular

React

Vue

Python

Node JS

PHP

The website may or may not contain back-end functionality. 

We encourage participants to use online resources for reference; however, we expect them to be able to explain the functionality of their code. The participants would be required to present their website and code to the judges on a live call (for approximately 10-15 minutes) to avoid any blind plagiarism.

Scoring Metric

For fair evaluation, participants’ entries will be judged on the basis of the criteria listed below.

User Experience

Aesthetics

Responsiveness and Reactiveness

Functionality (if applicable)

Submission

Before submission, make sure you register yourself for this event.

The submissions for this event are open till 48 hours after the topic is given (please refer to the time table for the exact time). The evaluation of any submission after the deadline is at Team Kontigence’s discretion.

We offer two submission options. You can archive your code in a zip file and email it to us. Alternatively, you can upload your code to Github and share a link with us. Our email address of submission is submissions@Kontigence.com.

Please ensure that the subject of your email is in the following format:

[Web Dev] Team Name, School Name

You will receive a confirmation email in 24 hours of submission. If you do not receive a confirmation email, please contact us.

Interview

After submission of your code, we will send you an email confirming your time preferences, platform preferences and ask for your phone number to remind you 30 minutes before the session. The live session will take place on either Skype, Microsoft Teams or Zoom.

Some judges may not be able to attend the live session due to different timezones. Because of this, the live sessions will be recorded so that those judges can also evaluate your website. There may be other members of team Kontigence on to ask questions on behalf of the absent judges also.

Resources

To ensure a level playing field, certain resources – such as stock pictures, access to icon libraries and design packs – would be provided to all the participants.

Free high-resolution images: https://unsplash.com/

Free Icons, Photos and Videos: https://icons8.com/

Free Vectors, Stock Photos and Icons: https://www.freepik.com/

Sample Design

We have spent ample time developing our own website, and you make take that as a sample of a good website design. This serves as no means, however, as a bar for the design, and participants are encouraged to cross limits to make their website as functional and as aesthetic as possible.
