Professor Proton

Participants will use common items to explain a scientific phenomena. The participants will film a video of the same and send us the entries, which will be judged.

Letter to the Participants

Dear Scientists,

It gives us immense pleasure to welcome you to Kontigence – Professor Proton. Many global issues emerge daily, most of which are ignored. Scientists have the unique trait of being able to find solutions for these problems, responsible for most of the benefits we take for granted in modern life. The world today has fallen into a state of chaos, and these same scientists are on the frontlines, defending the very essence of humanity’s survival. We rely on them now, and will rely on them in the future. You can take the step toward being part of he next generation of innovators that change the world.

Everyone has a scientist in them, and we want to give that scientist a chance. A part of you, curious about the workings of the universe and how it comes together to become what we know it to be. We aim to instigate a scientist in you, and we are sure you’ll enjoy the process, and perhaps, even consider higher studies in the field.

Let’s get sciencing!

Team Kontigence

Guidelines

All Participants are required to use items available in their house to design and perform an experiment which will be recorded and presented to make a video to demonstrate any scientific or mathematical concept of their choice in a compelling and comprehensive manner.

The videos are expected to be 5 to 10 minutes long.

The scope of the event allows for submission from the following disciplines:

Natural Sciences – Such as Physics and Chemistry;

Human Sciences – Such as Economics and Psychology;

Mathematics – Such as Computer Science and Arithmetic;

Please note that “Human Sciences” are also referred to as “Social Sciences” in some parts of the world.

At its core, this is a science competition, hence, participants will neither be penalized or awarded any additional marks for the picture quality of their videos. This is to ensure a level playing field for all participants.

Any submission containing any dangerous experiments will be disqualified; this rule has been placed for the safety of the participants.

Referencing online materials – for understanding the topic, providing proof for a statement or any other purpose – is allowed; however, plagiarism will not be tolerated; if found, then the participant would be disqualified and their institution will receive a letter from our team describing their malpractice and low sense of academic integrity.

Scoring Metric

For fair evaluation, participants’ entries will be judged on the basis of the criteria listed below.

Engagement – How well the audience’s interest is retained

Comprehensibility – How easy it is to understand the demonstration

Creativity – How innovative the explanation was

Complexity – How difficult the chosen topic was

Uniqueness – How different is the topic chosen compared to other participants

Each of these 5 categories will be graded out of 5, for a total of 25.

Submission

Before submission, make sure you register yourself for this event.

The submissions for this event are open till Friday the 22nd of May at 12pm GMT. The evaluation of any submission after the deadline is at Team Kontigence’s discretion.

We require you to upload the video to YouTube and share a link – public and unlisted links are both acceptable – with submissions@Kontigence.com

Please ensure that the subject of your email is in the following format:

[Professor Proton] Your Name, School Name

You will receive a confirmation email in 24 hours of submission. If you do not receive a confirmation email, please contact us.
