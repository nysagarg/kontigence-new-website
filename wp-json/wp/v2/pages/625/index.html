Letter to the Participants<\/h4>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

Dear Explorers,<\/p>

It gives us immense pleasure to welcome you to Kontigence – Cicada 6602.<\/p>

There are two fundamental methods of thought that one can apply when deciding to do something: purpose and curiosity. Acting through purpose means achieving a predetermined goal. Writing this letter is an act of purpose. Curiosity, however, is a more interesting perspective that is often underutilized. In taking on an activity because of curiosity, you aren\u2019t taking on a task despite an uncertain outcome, rather, you are taking on a task, because the result is uncertain. While acting from purpose comes from the need to achieve something known, curiosity drives us to learn something new and explore the magnificent world we live in.<\/p>

From an emotional perspective, curiosity can be frightening. That is why it killed the cat. In the primitive world, being too curious would certainly result in your death. Perhaps, that is the reason for the fear of the unknown we humans have. But we don\u2019t live in primitive times.<\/p>

To overcome the fear of curiosity and to establish your skills as an explorer of the new world – the online world – we implore you to embark on this journey with us, and prove that Generation Z has the ability to make a difference.\u00a0<\/p>

Guidelines<\/h4>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t

The participants would be registering for this event in teams of two to four members. A team name will be required during registration.<\/p>\n

All teams would be given certain starting instructions based on which they can take one of multiple routes to the final destination.<\/p>\n

All teams will have 2 days to try and solve as many stages as possible. The first team to reach the final destination wins. If no team is able to reach the final destination within the given time, then moderators will extend the time based on their analysis of the participants performance.<\/p>\n

We encourage multiple teams to work together to be able to solve the puzzles and would be setting up a discord server for all the teams to chat, where they can have private contact with other teams as well as broadcast a message to everyone else. All clues will also be released on the discord server.<\/p>\n

All teams are allowed to ask the moderators for one clue. If the moderators see that a certain puzzle is proving to be too difficult for all the participants, they may give additional hints. This is completely at the moderator’s discretion.<\/p>\n

Absolutely no knowledge of programming will be required for this event. However, knowledge of ciphers may come in handy.<\/p>\n
