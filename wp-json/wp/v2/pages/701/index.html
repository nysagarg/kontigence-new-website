Letter to the Participants<\/h4>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t

Dear Scientists,<\/p>

It gives us immense pleasure to welcome you to Kontigence – Creativity Clash.\u00a0Many global issues emerge daily, most of which are ignored. Scientists have the unique trait of being able to find solutions for these problems, responsible for most of the benefits we take for granted in modern life.\u00a0<\/span>The world today has fallen into a state of chaos, and these same scientists are on the frontlines, defending the very essence of humanity’s survival. We rely on them now, and will rely on them in the future. You can take the step toward being part of he next generation of innovators that change the world.<\/span><\/p>

Everyone has a scientist in them, and we want to give that scientist a chance. A part of you, curious about the workings of the universe and how it comes together to become what we know it to be. We aim to instigate a scientist in you, and we are sure you’ll enjoy the process, and perhaps, even consider higher studies in the field.<\/p>

Let’s get sciencing!<\/p>

Guidelines<\/h4>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t

All Participants are required to use items available in their house to design and perform an experiment which will be recorded and presented to make a video to demonstrate any scientific or mathematical concept of their choice in a compelling and comprehensive manner.<\/p>\n

The videos are expected to be 5 to 10 minutes long.<\/p>\n

The scope of the event allows for submission from the following disciplines:<\/p>\n

Natural Sciences – <\/strong>Such as Physics and Chemistry;<\/p>\n

Human Sciences –<\/strong> Such as Economics and Psychology;<\/p>\n

Mathematics – <\/strong>Such as Computer Science and Arithmetic;<\/p>\n

Please note that “Human Sciences” are also referred to as “Social Sciences” in some parts of the world.<\/p>\n

At its core, this is a science competition, hence, participants will neither be penalized or awarded any additional marks for the picture quality of their videos. This is to ensure a level playing field for all participants.<\/p>\n

Any submission containing any dangerous experiments will be disqualified<\/strong>; this rule has been placed for the safety of the participants.<\/p>\n

Referencing online materials – for understanding the topic, providing proof for a statement or any other purpose – is allowed; however, plagiarism will not be tolerated; if found, then the participant would be disqualified and their institution will receive a letter from our team describing their malpractice and low sense of academic integrity.<\/p><\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/section>\n\t\t\t\t

Scoring Metric<\/h4>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t

For fair evaluation, participants\u2019 entries will be judged on the basis of the criteria listed below.<\/p>

Engagement – <\/strong>How well the audience\u2019s interest is retained<\/p>

Comprehensibility –\u00a0<\/strong>How easy it is to understand the demonstration<\/span><\/p>

Creativity –<\/strong> How innovative the explanation was<\/p>

Complexity – <\/strong>How difficult the chosen topic was<\/p>

Uniqueness –<\/strong> How different is the topic chosen compared to other participants<\/p>

Each of these 5 categories will be graded out of 5, for a total of 25.<\/p><\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/section>\n\t\t\t\t
